# Week 4 Notes - Introduction to Large Language Models (LLMs)

## What are Large Language Models?

Large Language Models (LLMs) are advanced AI systems trained on massive amounts of text data.

Their primary objective is to understand language patterns and generate meaningful responses.

Examples of popular LLMs include:

- GPT
- Gemini
- Claude
- Llama

---

## Capabilities of LLMs

LLMs can perform various language-related tasks such as:

- Text Generation
- Question Answering
- Summarization
- Translation
- Information Extraction
- Report Writing

These capabilities make them useful across many industries.

---

## Basic Working of LLMs

At a high level, LLMs work by predicting the next most likely token in a sequence.

Process:

1. Input text is converted into tokens.
2. Tokens are transformed into numerical representations.
3. The model processes these representations.
4. The model predicts the next token.
5. Responses are generated one token at a time.

---

## Tokens

A token is a small unit of text processed by a language model.

Examples:

Text:

```text
Machine Learning is powerful.
```

Possible tokens:

```text
["Machine", "Learning", "is", "powerful"]
```

Tokens are the basic building blocks used by LLMs.

---

## Prompt Engineering

Prompt Engineering is the process of creating effective prompts that guide the model toward desired outputs.

A well-designed prompt usually produces better results.

---

## Types of Prompting

### Zero-Shot Prompting

The model receives only the task.

Example:

```text
Explain Machine Learning.
```

---

### Role-Based Prompting

The model is assigned a specific role.

Example:

```text
You are a quality inspection engineer.
Explain a product defect.
```

---

### Few-Shot Prompting

The model is provided with examples before performing a task.

This helps improve consistency and accuracy.

---
